Relational Databases

Entity

  • An object, person, event or thing of interest about which data is to be recorder
  • e.g. Customer
  • e.g. Order

Attribute

  • A property of an entity
  • e.g. Cutomer ID
  • e.g. Customer Name

Relationship

  • Link or association between two entities
  • Customer can make many orders, but each order is only made by one customer.

Types of Relationship

  • One-to-one (1:1)
    1. Employee and company Car;
    2. Employee and company Car;

  • One-to-Many (1:n)
    1. Owner and Pet;
    2. Borrower and Book;

  • Many-to-Many (n:m)
    1. Student and Course;
    2. Film and Film Star;

Before you design a database you need to think about how it is going to be laid out. The design decisions are to some extent arbitrary. There is no “technical” reason to do one thing over another, it is down to your personal opinion.
